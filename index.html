About Me

I am a Senior Lecturer and Research Coordinator in the School of Information Technology at Whitecliffe College, Wellington. I hold a PhD in Computer Science from Victoria University of Wellington (thesis: Error Space Search for Combinatorial Optimisation), a Master of Engineering from Sejong University, Seoul, and a BSc from the University of Science — Ho Chi Minh City National University.

My research sits at the intersection of combinatorial optimisation, nature-inspired computation, and applied machine learning. On the theoretical side, I design and analyse metaheuristic algorithms — tabu search, evolutionary strategies, and swarm intelligence — for NP-hard problems in scheduling, logistics, portfolio selection, and software engineering. On the applied side, I develop machine learning models for real-world challenges: housing demand forecasting, toxicity detection for educational chatbots, healthcare survival prediction, and sentiment analysis for e-commerce. A unifying concern across both strands is rigorous experimental methodology: I evaluate algorithms using non-parametric statistical tests (Wilcoxon, Friedman, Nemenyi) and benchmark against established baselines to produce results that are reproducible and genuinely comparable.

A growing focus of my work is the application of explainable AI (XAI) to long-term cancer survivorship prediction, developed in collaboration with clinical specialists in New Zealand (surgeons, oncologists) and international researchers at the University of Auckland and the University of Leeds. This programme is grounded in real patient data and aims at tools that clinicians can meaningfully interpret and act upon.

Prior to Whitecliffe, I served as Deputy Head of the Department of Information Technology at the Industrial University of Ho Chi Minh City (IUH), where I led curriculum development towards ABET accreditation (achieved 2020). I also serve as AI Advisor for AIlogy Vietnam and Academic Advisor for DataDude Vietnam, bridging academic research and industry.

Research Interests

Combinatorial Optimisation & Metaheuristics

Designing adaptive metaheuristic algorithms — Tabu Search, Iterated Local Search, evolutionary strategies, swarm intelligence — for NP-hard problems including the Quadratic Assignment Problem, Discounted Knapsack Problem, Set Covering Problem, and cardinality-constrained portfolio selection. Current work includes Adaptive Failsafe Tabu Search (AFTS) with a novel diversification mechanism and correlation-driven local search for portfolio optimisation.

Tabu Search QAP ILS Knapsack Portfolio Selection
Explainable AI in Healthcare

Applying explainable AI to long-term breast cancer survivorship prediction using real New Zealand patient data, in collaboration with clinical specialists and researchers at the University of Auckland and the University of Leeds (NIHR Centre for Doctoral Training in Medical AI). Aimed at producing interpretable, clinician-actionable decision support tools — not black-box predictions.

XAI Cancer Survivorship Clinical Decision Support
Applied ML & NLP

Data-centric NLP for Vietnamese educational toxicity detection (ViMSTox), fine-tuning LLMs for e-commerce sentiment analysis (IEEE Access), and housing demand forecasting with ensemble methods (Elastic Net, XGBoost, RNN). Research focused on social-impact domains where data quality and domain adaptation are critical challenges.

NLP LLM Fine-Tuning Forecasting Data-Centric AI
Software Engineering Analytics

Novel metrics and hybrid loss functions for software effort estimation, including the SIG metric for evaluating estimation bias and SIG-integrated loss functions for multi-layer perceptron models. International collaboration with researchers in Vietnam and the Czech Republic, targeting IEEE Transactions on Software Engineering.

Effort Estimation Hybrid Loss MLP
Nature-Inspired Computation

Bio-inspired algorithms — Binary Salp Swarm, Social Spider Algorithm, Genetic Algorithms — for combinatorial and continuous optimisation, published in PLOS ONE and Scopus-indexed venues. Current exploration of GAN-based solvers for NP-hard problems, investigating deep generative models as alternatives to classical heuristics.

Swarm Intelligence Evolutionary Computation GAN Solvers
IoT & Network Optimisation

Spectrum allocation in cognitive radio networks, adaptive handover for mobile sensor networks, IoT integration standards (MATTER, IEEE ICETAS 2024), and earlier work on security protocols for wireless sensor networks and the IEEE 802.16j relay standard (Sejong University).

IoT Cognitive Radio Wireless Security
